Most importantly: what about noise reduction?


Very cool even against the background of any headphones with active noise reduction: - plugs with a snug fit or overhead. Compared to standard AirPods, these are generally space. You hold down the button, the speakers say ‚Äúdust!‚Äù And you find yourself in a vacuum.

At AirPods Pro noise reduction is arranged differently than most competitors. At first, everything is as usual: the microphone picks up noise from the outside, and it is compensated by a backward sound wave. But then another microphone directed inward of the ear makes fine tuning, and the rest of the noise is suppressed again.



An example from life. On regular AirPods, listening to podcasts on the subway is not possible. In the "firmware" - completely, and even the volume does not need to be twisted. The noise of the street is not heard at all, in the Pro store the background music is almost completely cut off, and in the transport, the rumble is distinguishable, but very quiet.

Passive noise reduction, perhaps, at the level of any other "plugs". Only I do not see any reason not to include active, because it does not noticeably affect the operating time. Although in the settings this feature can be completely cut down, you can nevertheless: so that no noise reduction or transparency mode work.

A nice bonus is the valve, which relieves excess air pressure between the membrane and the earpiece. Usually my ears "itch" from this, but in these I have not noticed yet.

And what is this transparent mode?


This feature was made as opposed to noise reduction - in this mode, the sounds outside reach the eardrums not through the silicone inserts, but through the microphone and speaker of the headphones. At the same time, the upper and middle frequencies are even slightly amplified. It turns out that the headphones can not be removed. But if music is turned on, it still clogs all the sounds from the outside - you won‚Äôt be able to pause it.



And, by the way, in the "firmware" you hear your voice not as if from under the water, but as if there were no headphones. They say that Apple worked on this specially, tuning microphones.

Is the new management convenient?


Previously, you could just touch the outside of the headphone and switch the track or pause. Now the control was moved to the legs. At the same time, the legs became shorter, and the vibration sensors were replaced by pressure sensors. It is necessary not strongly, but precisely to squeeze the ends of the "flashing" so that they understand what you want from them. The first two or three days were uncomfortable, and I missed, and then I learned to grab the leg the first time, and it became quite ok - no worse than before.


This flat area needs to be pressed

  • You squeeze it briefly once - put the track on pause or vice versa start it.
  • Two times - the next song.
  • Three times - the previous one.
  • Long press - toggles between transparent mode and noise reduction. If necessary, in the settings you can add a third state, when both are disabled. Then the modes will switch cyclically.

All this, of course, can be controlled from the phone. Switch tracks - as usual, and transparency and noise reduction are hidden in the volume slider. By a long press on it, three corresponding buttons appear.

Do they work with Android?
